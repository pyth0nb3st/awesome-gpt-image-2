Full Prompt

Create a wide 16:9 original derived-play concept scene titled "Derived Play: Patch Cable League".

Purpose: derive a playable prompt-engineering game from the patchbay use-case idea, but make it a distinct physical play object: a vertical magnetic patch-cable challenge cabinet with route slots, score meters, fuse tokens, checksum badges, and tiny output screens. Do not make a 2x3 board, a card spread, a tabletop route map, a poster, a loom, a projector, a printshop, a weather station, a planetarium, a film stage, or a pinball table.

Scene format: a close diagonal view of a freestanding arcade-like modular signal cabinet in a fictional image lab. Players are absent. The cabinet has colored patch cords, removable constraint plugs, waveform tiles, small thumbnail screens, sliding gates, fuse indicators, and a scoring column. All brands, logos, people, organizations, and labels are fictional or absent.

Six derived play modes should be visible as mechanisms on the same cabinet, each with a short readable label:
1. "cable route draft" - draw three prompt fragments and route them through compatible signal ports.
2. "overload fuse" - avoid tripping a fuse by removing one conflicting instruction before generation.
3. "checksum badge" - earn a badge when a fictional object keeps the same silhouette across a series.
4. "diff trace duel" - choose which iteration changed only the intended visual feature.
5. "thumbnail gate" - pass a generated idea through tiny, medium, and wide readability gates.
6. "silence plug" - place a negative constraint plug to remove an unwanted motif without adding a new one.

Visual language: polished tactile prototype, half arcade cabinet and half engineering test rig; graphite metal, warm off-white labels, teal/red/yellow/green cords, glowing meters, crisp screens, small physical tokens; rich detail, organized hierarchy, playful but not cartoonish.

Composition: the full cabinet fills the frame from lower left to upper right; cable paths visibly connect the six mechanisms. The output should feel interactive and rule-based, with no hands, no humans, and no real-world brand marks.

Safety and originality constraints: original concept only; no external prompt or image source; all entities, marks, characters, places, labels, and institutions must be fictional or rights-safe; avoid disallowed sensitive, unsafe, adult, graphic, real-world identity, brand-restricted, character-restricted, living-artist-style, and watermark material.

Do not include: underwater sonar, bathymetry, portholes, reference locks, mask rings, crop windows, compression tests, 2x3 research board, tabletop card spread, greenhouse, loom, weather room, letterpress, planetarium, lantern slide stack, film set, airport terminal, laundromat, pinball table, or star map.
