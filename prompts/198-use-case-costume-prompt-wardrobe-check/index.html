Full Prompt

Create one polished public-gallery concept image titled "Use Case Costume: Prompt Wardrobe Check".

Output format: a single vertical 4:5 fashion atelier fitting photograph, not a grid, not a contact sheet, not a board game, not a poster, not a textile toile, not a dress pattern sheet, not a puppet rig, not a choreography score, not a film stage, not a product catalog, not a library cabinet, not a courtroom, not an elevator, not a music box, not an optical bench, not a watch, not a greenhouse, not a tasting counter, not a fossil drawer, not a toy latch.

Scene: a fictional stage-costume fitting alcove with one empty adjustable mannequin wearing a modular fantasy explorer outfit made from neutral rights-safe shapes: detachable cape panels, sleeve cuffs, utility sash, soft boots, mirrored collar plates, and removable embroidered patches. No people, no faces, no bodies beyond the mannequin form. The set is unbranded and invented.

Mechanism: GPT Image 2 prompt design is expressed as wardrobe continuity checking instead of a machine. Show these readable labels as small sewn tabs, mirror labels, and brass tags: Silhouette Lock, Reference Swatch, Patch Edit, Caption Embroidery, Material Truth, Scale Mirror, Pose Rail, Continuity Hook, Safety Wardrobe. The silhouette lock is a translucent outline behind the mannequin; reference swatches sit on a small rail; patch edits are detachable patch zones; caption accuracy is tested by embroidered text tabs; material truth is shown with fabric/metal/leather-like sample cards that are clearly synthetic props; scale mirror is a gridded full-length mirror; pose rail shows three tiny stance markers; continuity hooks hold matching cape and boot pieces.

Visual language: premium editorial atelier photography, warm oak, matte ivory mannequin, graphite mirror grid, muted teal and marigold tags, soft studio light, crisp shallow shadows, tactile close-up detail, short readable labels. Everything is fictional, original, unbranded, rights-safe, non-political, non-adult, non-graphic, and harmless. Avoid real marks, public figures, recognizable characters, celebrity likenesses, living-artist imitation, unsafe instructions, explicit material, graphic harm, and watermarks.
