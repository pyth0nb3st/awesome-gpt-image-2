Full Prompt

Create one polished public-gallery image titled "Derived Play: Wardrobe Continuity Relay".

This is a derived playable mode from Prompt Wardrobe Check, but it must look like a different rules object: a cooperative costume-continuity relay built around a rotating wardrobe carousel, not a fitting alcove, not a single mannequin portrait, not a textile toile, not a dress pattern sheet, not a puppet rig, not a choreography score, not a film stage, not a 2x3 grid, not cards, not a contact sheet, not a catalog cabinet, not a toy latch, not a fossil drawer, not a board game rectangle.

Output format: one 3:2 overhead-oblique tabletop product photograph of a circular miniature wardrobe carousel on a neutral studio table. The carousel has eight radial wardrobe bays, small faceless mannequin pegs, detachable cape tiles, cuff rings, sash tokens, embroidered label strips, fabric swatch coins, mirror wedges, and three continuity hooks. No people, no hands, no faces. All costume elements are fictional, unbranded, and rights-safe.

Playable rules shown through objects: players rotate the carousel one bay per turn, draw a Silhouette Token, attach one Patch Tile, trade one Swatch Coin, pass through the Mirror Wedge, place one Caption Strip, and hang matching pieces on the Continuity Hooks. A round scores only when the same invented explorer outfit remains recognizable across three mannequin pegs while one allowed patch edit changes and every caption strip is readable.

Readable short labels on components: Carousel Turn, Silhouette Token, Patch Tile, Swatch Coin, Mirror Wedge, Caption Strip, Hook Match, Outfit Echo, Safety Pocket. Visual language: premium tactile tabletop prototype, warm walnut carousel, ivory mannequin pegs, muted teal cloth, marigold tokens, graphite labels, brass hardware, crisp shadows, playful but sophisticated. Everything is fictional, original, unbranded, rights-safe, non-political, non-adult, non-graphic, and harmless. Avoid real marks, public figures, recognizable characters, celebrity likenesses, living-artist imitation, unsafe instructions, explicit material, graphic harm, and watermarks.
