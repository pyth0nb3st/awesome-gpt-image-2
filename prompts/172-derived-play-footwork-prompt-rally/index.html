Derived play concept: Footwork Prompt Rally. Show a playable tabletop-floor hybrid game where players draft image prompts by moving abstract shoe-print tokens across a compact rehearsal mat. The mat is not a 2x3 board and not a circular arena: it is an irregular hopscotch-like path shaped like a bent lightning bolt, with embedded beat counters, pivot hinges, pause wells, crop gates, style tempo sliders, and a final Safety Bow finish pad.

Game mechanics to depict as readable short labels on the mat: Step Draft, Pivot Steal, Reference Tap, Mask Sidestep, Caption Rest, Tempo Swap, Crop Gate, Bow Score. Add small non-branded tokens: colored footprint pieces, blank prompt slips, tiny thumbnail tiles, beat chips, and neutral score clips. The rules should feel like a prompt-building movement game: every step changes subject, context, reference strength, edit mask, caption clarity, style speed, frame boundary, or safety review.

Composition: one high-detail isometric scene on a studio table/floor surface; no six-panel research board, no cards-only spread, no magnets, no neon, no watch gears, no puppet strings, no abacus beads, no microfluidic channels, no greenhouse, no weather station. Make it clearly derived from the choreography score but mechanically more game-like and interactive.

Visual style: premium photographed prototype, tactile cork mat, painted rehearsal tape, brass beat chips, translucent acrylic gates, small paper slips, crisp shadows, playful but disciplined. Palette: cork tan, charcoal, brass, coral, cyan, leaf green, and white.

Safety and originality constraints: fictional objects only; no real brands, no logos, no trademarks, no celebrities, no public figures, no copyrighted characters, no political persuasion, no elections or parties, no harmful instructions, no weapons, no sexual or nude content, no gore or graphic violence, no living-artist style imitation, no watermark.
