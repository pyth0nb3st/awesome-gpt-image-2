Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: derived play image from Prompt Tube Bending, fictional neon routing game artifact, 3:4 portrait
Primary request: Create a polished original image titled "Derived Play: Glow Circuit Rodeo" showing a tabletop light-routing game derived from GPT Image 2 prompt tube-bending experiments. It must not look like a 2x3 research board, card spread, app UI, printing press, loom, paper scroll, microfluidic maze, abacus, marionette rig, origami court, hotel cabinet, ice rack, kiln, mosaic wall, pinball table, or standard board-game grid.
Scene/backdrop: overhead editorial photo of a dark matte tabletop with a modular fictional neon-tube route made of safe unpowered glowing acrylic segments, small insulated-looking tokens, color caps, bridge clips, crop rings, and paper provenance tags. No real brand marks.
Game mechanism: players build one continuous glow route through abstract prompt checkpoints; they draft bends, bid on hue caps, bridge around blockers, test letter clarity, manage glow spill, keep the route inside crop halos, and finish with a tag seal.
Visible rule stations integrated as physical pieces along the route, not equal panels: "Bend Draft" picks a curved tube segment, "Hue Bet" locks color influence, "Bridge Clip" jumps over an obstacle, "Letter Check" scores a readable word tile, "Spill Tax" pays for excess glow, "Halo Keep" keeps the route inside frame rings, and "Seal Finish" completes the safe source tag.
Visual language: premium handmade prototype photography, cyan amber magenta glow, clear acrylic and rubber feet, tactile labels, sophisticated playful design, dark bench with reflections.
Safety and originality: fictional unpowered light toy metaphor only; no real brands, no public figures, no political content, no weapons, no operational electrical instructions, no sexual content, no graphic violence, no copyrighted characters, no living-artist style.
Composition: portrait orientation; a continuous glowing route loops diagonally through irregular checkpoints with tokens and tags; visually related to the neon use-case but clearly a separate game format.
