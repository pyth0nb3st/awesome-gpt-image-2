Full Prompt

Create a wide 16:9 playable QA puzzle sheet titled in small clear text "Derived Play: Chapter Mismatch Relay". It is derived from Episode Visual Kit Forge: players inspect a fictional podcast episode visual kit and repair mismatches before publishing.

Scene/backdrop: a flat editorial QA poster pinned to a neutral production-room wall, crisp readable labels, not a product UI screenshot, not a tabletop game, not a physical controller or lab instrument.

Main layout: a large horizontal episode timeline across the middle for the fictional show "Field Notes From Tomorrow" with five chapter stops: 00:00 Cold Open, 04:20 Host Intro, 10:40 Guest Idea, 18:15 Listener Q&A, 27:30 Recap. Around the timeline are movable-looking but printed asset cards: chapter image cards, quote cards, speaker tags H/G, audiogram waveform slices, and show-note fallback snippets.

Game mechanics: intentionally mark six safe fictional errors with numbered warning badges and readable labels: 1 WRONG IMAGE, 2 QUOTE DRIFT, 3 SPEAKER SWAP, 4 TIME GAP, 5 FALLBACK MISSING, 6 TINY TEXT. On the right, show a repair relay column with steps: SPOT, MATCH, PATCH, PREVIEW, PASS. Along the bottom, show before/after mini strips for each fix and a final publish gate stamped "READY".

Visual details: use abstract graphic episode art, waveform lanes, timestamp pins, colored thread lines connecting cards to chapters, clear checkboxes, small magnifier icons, and contrast/readability chips. Make it obvious this is a game about visual-generation consistency, metadata alignment, and multi-format asset QA.

Style: polished media-ops diagram, playful but professional, varied palette with navy, teal, plum, gold, coral, and off-white, high contrast, readable text, compact layout that does not resemble a 2x2 or 2x3 contact sheet.

Safety and originality constraints: no real podcast, no real host or guest, no faces or portraits, no platform logos, no real brands, no copyrighted shows, no celebrities, no political topics, no elections or parties, no true-crime violence imagery, no weapons, no harmful instructions, no medical/legal/financial claims, no adult or explicit content, no gore, no living-artist style imitation, no copied UI, no copied prompt text, no source image reuse.
